Basic Vocabulary

Term: a number, variable, or a number joined with a variable
  • 4y
  • 10x
  • x - this is considered a term because it has an invisible coefficient of 1 (1x)
  • 5 - this is considered a term because it can be written as 5x⁰
Polynomial: a combination of terms connected by addition or subtraction
Example: 4x² + 10x - 5
Monomial: one term
Example: 5x
Binomial: two terms joined by addition or subtraction
Example: y - 2x
Trinomial: three terms joined by addition or subtraction
Example: 4x² - 10x - 5

Adding and Subtracting Polynomials

When adding or subtracting polynomials, combine like terms. Like terms have the same variable and the same exponent.

Examples of like terms:
  • 3x² and 2x²
  • 5x and -7x
Examples of unlike terms:
  • 3x² and 3x
  • 4x and 4y

Adding Polynomials

Example:

(3x² + 5x - 4) + (2x² - 7x + 9)

Step 1: Remove the parentheses.

3x² + 5x - 4 + 2x² - 7x + 9

Step 2: Combine like terms.

3x² + 2x² = 5x²

5x - 7x = -2x

-4 + 9 = 5

Final Answer: 5x² - 2x + 5

Subtracting Polynomials

Example:

(6x² + 4x - 3) - (2x² - 5x + 7)

Step 1: Distribute the negative sign to every term in the second polynomial.

6x² + 4x - 3 - 2x² + 5x - 7

Step 2: Combine like terms.

6x² - 2x² = 4x²

4x + 5x = 9x

-3 - 7 = -10

Final Answer: 4x² + 9x - 10

Multiplying Monomials and Polynomials

To multiply a monomial by a polynomial, use the distributive property and multiply the outside term by every term inside the parentheses.

Example:

5x(4x³ + 2y - 1)

Step 1: Multiply 5x by each term.

5x · 4x³ = 20x⁴

5x · 2y = 10xy

5x · -1 = -5x

Final Answer: 20x⁴ + 10xy - 5x

Multiplying Binomials

To multiply binomials, multiply each term in the first binomial by each term in the second binomial.

Example:

(x + 3)(x + 5)

Step 1: Multiply each term.

x · x = x²

x · 5 = 5x

3 · x = 3x

3 · 5 = 15

Step 2: Put everything together.

x² + 5x + 3x + 15

Step 3: Combine like terms.

5x + 3x = 8x

Final Answer: x² + 8x + 15

Multiplying a Trinomial and a Binomial

Example:

(x + 2)(x² + 3x - 4)

Step 1: Multiply x by every term in the trinomial.

x · x² = x³

x · 3x = 3x²

x · -4 = -4x

Step 2: Multiply 2 by every term in the trinomial.

2 · x² = 2x²

2 · 3x = 6x

2 · -4 = -8

Step 3: Put everything together.

x³ + 3x² - 4x + 2x² + 6x - 8

Step 4: Combine like terms.

3x² + 2x² = 5x²

-4x + 6x = 2x

Final Answer: x³ + 5x² + 2x - 8

Factoring Out a GCF

GCF stands for greatest common factor. The GCF is the biggest factor that every term has in common. To factor out a GCF, divide every term by the GCF and place the GCF outside the parentheses.

Example 1

12x³ + 18x² + 6x

Step 1: Find the GCF of the numbers.

12, 18, 6

The greatest common factor is 6.

Step 2: Find the variable they all share.

x³, x², x

They all have at least one x.

Step 3: Put them together.

6x

Step 4: Factor out 6x.

12x³ + 18x² + 6x

6x(2x² + 3x + 1)

Final Answer: 6x(2x² + 3x + 1)

Example 2: Finding the GCF with Prime Factors

Sometimes the GCF is not obvious. In that case, break each number into prime factors.

24x³ + 36x² + 60x

Step 1: Break down each coefficient.

24 = 2 · 2 · 2 · 3

36 = 2 · 2 · 3 · 3

60 = 2 · 2 · 3 · 5

Step 2: Find what they all have in common.

They all share:

2 · 2 · 3 = 12

Step 3: Find the variable GCF.

x³, x², x

They all share at least one x.

Step 4: Put them together.

12x

Step 5: Factor out 12x.

24x³ + 36x² + 60x

12x(2x² + 3x + 5)

Final Answer: 12x(2x² + 3x + 5)

Important note: if you’re unsure what the GCF is, using the guess and check method is your best bet; however, breaking down each coefficient into their prime factor is the formal method

Prime Numbers

A prime number is a number greater than 1 that can only be divided evenly by 1 and itself.

Examples: 2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13, 17

Factoring ax² + bx + c When a = 1

When a = 1, there is no visible coefficient in front of x².

Example:

x² + 7x + 12

Here:

a = 1, b = 7, c = 12

Step 1: Find two numbers that multiply to 12 and add to 7.

3 · 4 = 12

3 + 4 = 7

So the numbers are 3 and 4.

Step 2: Split the middle term.

x² + 3x + 4x + 12

Step 3: Group the terms.

(x² + 3x) + (4x + 12)

Step 4: Factor out the GCF from each group.

x(x + 3) + 4(x + 3)

Step 5: The parentheses should match. Now factor out the common parentheses.

(x + 4)(x + 3)

Final Answer: (x + 4)(x + 3)

Factoring ax² + bx + c When a ≠ 1

When a ≠ 1, there is a visible coefficient in front of x².

Example:

2x² + 7x + 3

Here:

a = 2, b = 7, c = 3

Step 1: Multiply a and c.

2 · 3 = 6

Step 2: Find two numbers that multiply to 6 and add to 7.

6 · 1 = 6

6 + 1 = 7

So the numbers are 6 and 1.

Step 3: Split the middle term.

2x² + 6x + x + 3

Step 4: Group the terms.

(2x² + x) + (6x + 3)

Step 5: Factor out the GCF from each group.

x(2x + 1) + 3(2x + 1)

Step 6: The parentheses should match. Now factor out the common parentheses.

(x + 3)(2x + 1)

Final Answer: (x + 3)(2x + 1)

Quick Rule Sheet

Topic Rule
Term A number, variable, or number joined with a variable
Polynomial Terms connected by addition or subtraction
Monomial 1 term
Binomial 2 terms
Trinomial 3 terms
Like Terms Same variable and same exponent
Adding Polynomials Combine like terms
Subtracting Polynomials Distribute the negative (if needed), then combine like terms
Multiplying Monomial by Polynomial Distribute the monomial to every term
Multiplying Binomials Multiply every term in the first binomial by every term in the second
GCF Biggest factor every term shares
Factoring out GCF Divide every term by the GCF and place it outside parentheses
Factoring when a = 1 Find two numbers that multiply to c and add to b
Factoring when a ≠ 1 Multiply a · c, then find two numbers that multiply to (a · c) and add to b
