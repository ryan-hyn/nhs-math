Real Numbers

Real numbers are all numbers that can be placed on a number line.
They include positive numbers, negative numbers, fractions, decimals, and square roots.

Types of Real Numbers

Type Description Examples
Natural Numbers Counting numbers starting from 1 1, 2, 3, 4
Whole Numbers Natural numbers including 0 0, 1, 2, 3
Integers Positive and negative whole numbers -3, -1, 0, 4
Rational Numbers Numbers that can be written as a fraction of two integers 1/2, -7/3, 0.25
Irrational Numbers Numbers that cannot be written as a simple fraction π, √2
Note: √-1 is not a real number. It is an imaginary number because you cannot find the square root of an negative number.

Order of Operations

Use PEMDAS:


Letter Meaning
P Parentheses
E Exponents
MD Multiplication & Division
AS Addition & Subtraction

Multiplication and division are solved from left to right. Addition and subtraction are also solved from left to right. This means that even though in PEMDAS, multiplication comes before division, if the division sign appears first in an equation, you would divide first. The same rule applies to addition and subtraction.

Examples:
7 ÷ 9 × 8 → divide first, then multiply

54 - 27 + 8 × 5 → multiply first, then solve subtraction and addition from left to right.

Variables and Expressions

Variables are letters used to represent unknown numbers.


Expression Meaning
40y 40 times y
40/y 40 divided by y
40 + y 40 plus y
40 - y 40 minus y

The number attached to a variable is called the coefficient.

Examples:
40y ÷ 5 = 8y → the coefficients are 40 and 8
3y × 20 = 60y → the coefficients are 3 and 60

When Terms Cannot Combine

You cannot add or subtract unlike terms because they either represent different values or because of PEMDAS.

Examples:
7x - 8 cannot simplify further because next in PEMDAS is multiplication
25x - 50y cannot simplify further not only because of PEMDAS, but because x and y are worth two different values

The Distributive Property

Using the distributive property, a number outside the parentheses multiplies with every term inside.

Forumula: a(b + c) = ab + ac

Examples:
4(50x + 200) = 200x + 800
2(x + 5) = 2x + 10

Factoring

Factoring is the reverse of distributing. Factoring only works when you factor or take out a common factor

Formula: ax + ay + az = a(x + y + z)

Example:
3y + 12x + 9 = 3(y + 4x + 3)

Combining Like Terms

Terms containing the same variable and exponent can be combined together.

Adding and Subtracting Like Terms

Examples:
4x + 4x = 8x
10k^5 - 3k^5 = 7k^5
8y^2 - 4y cannot be combined because y is raised to two separate powers
