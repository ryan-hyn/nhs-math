Unit 1: Foundations of Algebra

Real Numbers

Real numbers are all numbers that can be placed on a number line. They include positive numbers, negative numbers, fractions, decimals, and roots.

Types of Real Numbers

Type Description Examples
Natural Numbers Counting numbers starting from 1 1, 2, 3, 4
Whole Numbers Natural numbers including 0 0, 1, 2, 3
Integers Positive and negative whole numbers -3, -1, 0, 4
Rational Numbers Numbers that can be written as a fraction of two integers 1/2, -7/3, 0.25
Irrational Numbers Numbers that cannot be written as a simple fraction π, √2
Note: √-1 is not a real number. It is an imaginary number.

Order of Operations

Use PEMDAS:

Letter Meaning
P Parentheses
E Exponents
MD Multiplication & Division
AS Addition & Subtraction
Multiplication and division are solved from left to right. Addition and subtraction are also solved from left to right.
Examples:
7 ÷ 9 × 8 → divide first, then multiply

54 - 27 + 8 × 5 → multiply first, then left to right

Variables and Expressions

Variables are letters used to represent unknown numbers.

Expression Meaning
40y 40 times y
40/y 40 divided by y
40 + y 40 plus y
40 - y 40 minus y

The number attached to a variable is called the coefficient.

Examples:
40y ÷ 5 = 8y
3y × 20 = 60y

Why Some Terms Cannot Combine

You cannot add or subtract unlike terms because they represent different values.

7x - 8 cannot simplify further
25x - 50y cannot simplify further

The Distributive Property

a(b + c) = ab + ac
4(50x + 200) = 200x + 800
2(x + 5) = 2x + 10

Factoring

3y + 12x + 9 = 3(y + 4x + 3)

Combining Like Terms

4x + 4x = 8x
10k - 3k = 7k

Multiplying and Dividing Variables

xᵃ × xᵇ = xᵃ⁺ᵇ
5y × 8y = 40y²
xᵃ ÷ xᵇ = xᵃ⁻ᵇ
56y ÷ 7y = 8
