Multiplying Powers with the Same Base

A base is the number or variable being raised to a power → In 7², the base is 7.

When multiplying powers with the same base, add the exponents.

xᵃ × xᵇ = xᵃ⁺ᵇ

Example

7² × 7⁴

Since the bases are both 7, add the exponents:

7²⁺⁴ = 7⁶

7⁶ = 117,649

Final Answer: 117,649

Division Properties of Exponents

When dividing powers with the same base, subtract the exponents.

xᵃ ÷ xᵇ = xᵃ⁻ᵇ

Example

4⁵ ÷ 4³

Since the bases are both 4, subtract the exponents:

4⁵⁻³ = 4²

4² = 16

Final Answer: 16

Raising a Quotient to a Power

A quotient is the answer to a division problem. A fraction is also a quotient.

When raising a fraction to a power, raise both the numerator and denominator to that power.

(a/b)ⁿ = aⁿ / bⁿ

Example

(x/3)² = 16

Raise both the top and bottom to the second power:

x² / 3² = 16

x² / 9 = 16

Multiply both sides by 9:

x² = 144

Take the square root of both sides:

x = 12

Final Answer: x = 12

More Multiplication Properties of Exponents

Raising a Power to a Power

When raising a power to another power, multiply the exponents.

(aᵇ)ᶜ = aᵇᶜ

Example

(3²)⁴

Multiply the exponents:

3²ˣ⁴ = 3⁸

3⁸ = 6,561

Final Answer: 6,561

Simplifying an Expression with Powers

Example

(x³ × x⁵) ÷ x²

First, multiply the powers on top by adding exponents:

x³ × x⁵ = x³⁺⁵ = x⁸

Now divide:

x⁸ ÷ x² = x⁸⁻²

x⁶

Final Answer: x⁶

Simplifying a Product Raised to a Power

When a product is raised to a power, the outside exponent applies to every factor inside the parentheses.

(ab)ⁿ = aⁿbⁿ

Example

(2x³y)²

Apply the exponent to every factor:

(2)²(x³)²(y)²

Simplify each part:

2² = 4

(x³)² = x⁶

y² = y²

Put it all together:

Final Answer: 4x⁶y²

Zero Exponents

Any nonzero base raised to the power of zero equals 1.

x⁰ = 1, x ≠ 0

Example

4⁰ = 1

Why does x⁰ = 1?

Look at this pattern:

4³ = 64

4² = 16

4¹ = 4

4⁰ = 1

Each time the exponent goes down by 1, the answer is divided by 4. That is also why 0⁰ doesn't exist--you can't divide 0 by anything that gives you 1 as an answer.

Negative Exponents

A negative exponent means you flip the number into a fraction and make the exponent positive.

x⁻ⁿ = 1 / xⁿ

Example

2⁻⁵

Flip the fraction and change the sign of the exponent:

1 / 2⁵

2⁵ = 32

Final Answer: 1/32

Take a look:

10¹ = 10

10⁰ = 1

10⁻¹ = 1/10

10⁻² = 1/100

Each time the exponent goes down by 1, the answer is divided by 10 (the base).

While positive exponents multiply the base by itself repeatedly based on its value, negative exponents divide the base by itself repeatedly based on its value.

Evaluating an Expression Using Multiple Exponent Properties

Example

[(2³)((2⁴)²)(3⁰)] ÷ [(2²)(2⁻¹)]

Step 1: Simplify the power raised to a power.

(2⁴)² = 2⁴ˣ² = 2⁸

Step 2: Simplify the zero exponent.

3⁰ = 1

Now the expression becomes:

(2³)(2⁸)(1) ÷ (2²)(2⁻¹)

Step 3: Multiply powers in the numerator.

2³ × 2⁸ = 2³⁺⁸ = 2¹¹

Step 4: Multiply powers in the denominator.

2² × 2⁻¹ = 2²⁺⁽⁻¹⁾ = 2¹

Now the expression is:

2¹¹ ÷ 2¹

Step 5: Divide powers by subtracting exponents.

2¹¹⁻¹ = 2¹⁰

Step 6: Evaluate.

2¹⁰ = 1,024

Final Answer: 1,024
